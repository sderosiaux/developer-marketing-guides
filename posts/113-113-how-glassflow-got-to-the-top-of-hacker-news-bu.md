TLDR if you are planning to launch is:
  * Follow the guidelines, use the format that works, tone of voice (really this is key), spend time on tweaking the title [How to launch a dev tool on Hacker News](https://www.markepear.dev/blog/dev-tool-hacker-news-launch)
  * Spend a big (biggest) chunk talking about the problem and why obvious solutions don‚Äôt work. I explain exactly how folks from Tailscale do it time and time again in here [How to market on Hacker News (learnings from Tailscale)](https://www.markepear.dev/blog/developer-marketing-hacker-news)
  * Prepare for success. The last thing you want is to land on the first page and then meet people with bad experience. I wrote about it here: [How to create and audit your core developer journey](https://www.markepear.dev/blog/developer-journey)
  * Spend time explaining how it works, what you tried, why it didn‚Äôt work. ‚Äúhacker to hacker‚Äù. Use 700 words if this is what it takes to explain it. Some of the best launches [(like this one from fly.io](https://www.markepear.dev/example/fly-io-hacker-news-launch-description)) are long.

Since the things I wanted to show are interactive I ended up recording a video about it for the [pod](https://open.spotify.com/show/0wyxDG8BkClv5XltUiZdbD?si=e9cd97ab05394934&utm_source=newslepear.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=113-how-glassflow-got-to-the-top-of-hacker-news-two-ideas-from-buildkite-homepage-and-a-giveaway-inspiration). 
Two main inspirations are the social proof and the diagram/product section. 
**Hero social proof can be more than ‚Äújust logos‚Äù . The showcased mini case studies together with logos. 
  * Each showed: 
    * CICD jobs to anchor on product and build times, 
    * the result: a known app like Slack or Uber, 
    * and ‚ÄúROI‚Äù workflow improvement numbers like 60% faster etc.
  * Really tasteful and managed to convey so much info. 

**For the product sections (yes plural) they showed a single diagram. It showed various features/products/parts of their platform. 
  * As you scrolled you the Headline-subhead-CTA would change. 
  * And a different part of the platform was highlighted. 
  * I really loved this as showing diagrams together with text is hard. You either overwhelm with text or don‚Äôt say much.

### 3. Hand-iced cookies giveaway inspiration
So we wanted to do something funny, connected to product, unique for this event we organized a few weeks back in London. 
Ended up with giving people boxes of hand-iced cookies with machine learning curve charts on them. Something our users see every day in the product. 
And gifting everyone a box gives them an excuse/trigger to talk about the event/us at work the next day. Hopefully share in internal Slacks #random. 
Turned out pretty well I think. Some people took a few boxes to give to ‚Äúthis other company in our co-work‚Äù. Sweet.
